\section{Introduction and Rationale}
\subsection{Motivation}
Multi-branch tutoring centers operate in a highly dynamic environment where instructors frequently rotate between different branches to meet student demand. For these institutions, efficient scheduling is the operational backbone however, as the number of students, teachers, and physical branches grows, the complexity of coordinating weekly classes scales exponentially. Currently, administrators rely on manual systems to manage the schedule.

The booking process begins with a customer selecting a subject, followed by choosing a preferred instructor. The administrator must then manually filter through a static Excel spreadsheet to find and present that specific instructor's available time slots. If none of those times work for the customer, the administrator must repeat the entire filtering process for alternative instructors until a suitable time is found. Once a time is finally selected, the administrator acts as a middleman, sending a minimum of three separate LINE messages: first to the instructor to verify the requested slot, then back to the customer to finalize the booking, and finally to a staff group chat, So the class can be manually logged.

When an instructor is booked for consecutive classes at different branches, administrators must manually calculate and block out their standard 30-minute commute buffer. Managing these transition times by hand across dozens of instructors significantly slows down the booking process. Furthermore, without a branch capacity tracking system, branches are prone to overbooking, leading to situations where teachers and students arrive at a branch only to discover that there is no physical space available.

Ultimately, this repetitive workflow consumes an estimated 1 to 2 hours of administrative time each day and introduces significant risk of human error into the weekly recurring schedule. It forces administrators to manually cross-reference multiple tables within a massive, cluttered spreadsheet just to find a single available teacher for a requested branch.
\subsection{Problem Statement}
Administrators at multi-branch tutoring schools need an automated, commute-aware scheduling system because the current manual workflow wastes up to 2 hours of administrative time daily. The process requires sending at least three messages per booking, relies on a cluttered spreadsheet that obscures operational insights, and frequently results in branch overbooking. To resolve this, the administration requires an algorithmic system capable of automatically evaluating inter-branch travel times and making sure schedules is optimal.

\subsection{Purpose}
The primary purpose of the Converge application is to develop an automated scheduling and resource management system that highly mitigates the operational bottlenecks and inefficiencies associated with manual booking in multi-branch tutoring centers.

\subsection{Target Users}
The target users of Converge are categorized by their level of interaction with the system's core domain, primary users are the main operators who operate the system, while secondary users interact with peripheral interfaces to provide the essential data required for the system to function.

\subsubsection*{Primary Target Users}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Administrators are the primary operators who use the system daily to process parent requests. Their goal is to finalize complex, multi-branch schedules instantly while the customer is present. This automated workflow eliminates the operational risks of manual scheduling, specifically preventing branch overbooking and excessive wait times that could harm the school's reputation. Additionally, administrators utilize the system to manage the tutoring center's instructor roster and maintain up-to-date staff contact details.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection*{Secondary Target Users}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Instructors act as data providers for the system. They use a structured web form to submit their weekly availability rather than typing paragraph responses into Google Forms. Allowing the system to enforce commute buffers and generate conflict-free schedules for the administrative staff.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Project Scope}
This project focuses on the design and development of Converge, an enterprise-grade scheduling and resource management system utilizing heuristic optimization for multi-branch tutoring centers. The architecture replaces manual, error-prone workflows with an automated decision-support pipeline:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Automated Data Ingestion Form:} A structured web form that standardizes how instructors submit their availability, replacing unstructured manual entry on Google Form and ensuring clean data for the scheduling engine.
    \item \textbf{Constraint Satisfaction AI Engine:} A backend scheduling engine that balances variables like instructor availability and branch capacity to generate conflict-free schedules. It includes a transparent decision log, so administrators can easily understand how the engine arrived at its suggestions.
    \item \textbf{Travel Buffer System:} A configurable module that automatically calculates and applies physical commute times between branches. This ensures instructors are given travel windows without the system needing to rely on third-party mapping APIs.
    \item \textbf{Branch Capacity Monitoring:} A tracking module that compares concurrent bookings against the physical limits of each branch. It automatically flags full time slots to prevent overbooking and guarantee physical space for every student.
    \item \textbf{Converge Hub:} A centralized master calendar and administrative dashboard that replaces static spreadsheets. It allows administrators to view all multi-branch schedules at a glance, instantly filter by specific instructors, and securely manage the center's staff roster.
    \item \textbf{Automated Disaster Recovery:} A scheduled backup system that generates regular database snapshots, ensuring business continuity and rapid restoration in the event of a system failure.
\end{enumerate}
\textbf{Out of Scope}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Self-Service Client Booking:} The system will not include a self-booking system for parents. The client specifically wants administrators to handle scheduling directly, so they can negotiate and sell packages. Furthermore, based on established timetabling research which concludes that complex scheduling requires human intervention to guide the system\cite{schaerf1999}, Converge is designed to be an administrative decision-support tool rather than an app that automatically handles bookings behind the scenes.
    \item \textbf{Payment Gateway and Financial Processing:} The client already utilizes a proprietary application for financial processing, making a replacement unnecessary. Converge will operate strictly as a decoupled scheduling engine. Furthermore, because their existing application ledger lacks secure APIs, attempting to force an integration between the two systems introduces severe security risk.
    \item \textbf{Probabilistic Machine Learning / Predictive AI:} The system will not use Large Language Models or predictive AI. These models are prone to data hallucination when processing large datasets. Furthermore, they present severe architectural bottlenecks, self-hosting them might cause processing delays, while using third-party APIs might introduce high token costs. Therefore, the system strictly utilizes deterministic, rule-based AI.
    \item \textbf{Real-Time Geographic Mapping APIs:} The system will not integrate with third-party map services (e.g., Google Maps) to calculate live travel times. The client explicitly prefers using fixed, standardized travel durations between branches to maintain predictable daily schedules.
\end{enumerate}
